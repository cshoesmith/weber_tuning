Carburetor Family Overview

Family Typical Use Calculator Coverage
DCOE / sidedraft Individual-runner side-draft setups on naturally aspirated or period-style performance builds. Supports single, twin, and triple carb layouts, removable chokes, main jets, air correctors, idle jets, emulsion tubes, pump jets, auxiliary venturis, and needle valves.
IDF / downdraft Downdraft dual-throat performance carburetors on manifolds for upright or downdraft applications. Supports single and twin carb layouts, removable vents, main jets, air correctors, idle jets, emulsion tubes, pump jets, boosters, and needle valves.
32/36 progressive Street-oriented single-carb conversions with fixed progressive primary/secondary throttles. Supports common 32/36 models, fixed venturi pairs, primary/secondary mains, primary/secondary air correctors, idle jet, emulsion tube family, and needle valve suggestions.

The 32/36 mode models the common single progressive installation. Dual progressive setups are intentionally not modeled in this version because their linkage and manifold behavior vary wildly.

Shared Advisories

Reference Topic Range / Selection Advisory
Altitude Above sea level Baseline logic reduces the main jet by 2% per 1000 feet. A visible warning is shown above 2000 feet.
Temperature 40°F and below Colder air is denser, so real-world tuning often ends up slightly richer than the starting recommendation.
Temperature 41°F to 94°F Moderate temperature range; no extra numerical correction is applied.
Temperature 95°F and above Hotter air is thinner, so real-world tuning often ends up slightly leaner than the starting recommendation.
DCOE / IDF Body 40 body with venturi above 34mm Unusual pairing; verify signal strength and throttle response.
DCOE / IDF Body 45 body with venturi below 32mm or 44 IDF with very small vents Can work, but low-speed signal may be softer than a 40 DCOE.
DCOE / IDF Body 48 body with venturi below 36mm Usually happier with larger chokes and higher-RPM combinations.
32/36 Progressive Any listed model Primary barrels handle cruise and progression; secondary barrels come in later, so response depends heavily on linkage, manifold heat, and distributor curve.
